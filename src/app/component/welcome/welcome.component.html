Informed Consent Page

This research project explores how users would interact with software using other interaction means than the mouse and keyboard. In this study, we examine how users would perform common tasks in a spreadsheet application by drawing with digital ink on a touchscreen.

This app will show you several screenshots of spreadsheets and ask you how you would perform a series of operations in that simulated application. Please use the pen to draw your commands on the screen, and feel free to talk about your thoughts as you do so. The experiment will take about 30 minutes. In this study, we will collect the following data: A screen recording and digital ink of your pen strokes on the screen; an audio recording of your comments and conversations during the experiment; user experience feedback and demographic data.

All data collected during the experiment will be anonymized (i.e. associated with a neutral identification number), so it is not traceable to you or your organization. Parts of the anonymized data (excluding audio) may be published in individual or aggregate form.

You have the right to refuse to participate in this study or to withdraw your participation at any time without giving a reason, and without incurring any negative consequences, by tapping the red X button on top. Data collected before your withdrawal may be used with your consent. Once the research is complete, you may not forbid the use of data or demand that it be destroyed.

The principal investigators for this research project are Dr. Matthias Book, professor at the Department of Computer Science at the University of Iceland (book@hi.is), and Dr. Marc Hesenius, post-doctoral researcher at the Software Engineering Chair of the University of Duisburg-Essen, Germany (marc.hesenius@uni-due.de). This study is supported by the University of Iceland Research Fund (grant no. 92270).

If you have any questions about the experiment or your participation, please feel free to ask the researchers at any time before, during or after the experiment.

By tapping the Next Page button, you indicate that you have understood the above information and consent to participate in this study.
